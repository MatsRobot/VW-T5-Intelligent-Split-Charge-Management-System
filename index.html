🚐 VW T5 Intelligent Split-Charge Management

A precision Power Management Layer for VW T5/T5.1 Transporters. This project integrates an Arduino Nano with an LTC3780 Buck-Boost controller to actively regulate the current flow to modern leisure batteries, preventing the "low-resistance trap" common in upgraded electrical systems.

Important Compatibility: This system is designed specifically for VW Transporters with traditional (non-smart) alternators. It corrects the high-amperage surge that occurs when legacy alternators meet modern, low-resistance battery chemistry.

The Engineering Problem: The Low-Resistance Trap

When you replace an old lead-acid battery in a VW T5 with a modern AGM, Gel, or Lithium version, you introduce a new variable: significantly lower internal resistance.

Standard Voltage Sensitive Relays (VSR) create a "dumb" mechanical bridge. Because of the low resistance of new batteries, they attempt to pull massive current from the alternator—often exceeding 30A. This overloads the vehicle's original 20A wiring, repeatedly blowing fuses and potentially warping the battery plates due to rapid, unregulated heat buildup. Unless you limit the current, you risk damaging your expensive new investment.

Hardware Implementation & "Watchdog" Safety

Precision Dual-Rail Sensing

The system utilizes two INA219 High-Side sensors to monitor the input (Alternator) and output (Battery) rails independently. To handle the high-current demands, we replaced the stock shunt resistors on these modules with 0.01 Ohm (R100) shunts. This modification allows the Arduino Nano to calculate real-time wattage and conversion efficiency with laboratory-grade accuracy.

Active Current Limiting (LTC3780)

At the heart of the power stage is the LTC3780 Automatic Buck-Boost converter. We selected this specific module because it can flawlessly step voltage both up and down, maintaining a steady 14.4V even if the alternator voltage sags. By manually adjusting the hardware Constant Current (CC) trimmer to 14A, we ensure the battery never pulls more than the vehicle's loom can safely provide.

NE555 "Missing Pulse" Watchdog

To ensure zero parasitic drain and fail-safe operation, we integrated an NE555 Timer circuit. The Arduino sends a 1Hz "heartbeat" pulse to the 555; if the software hangs or the engine stops, the pulse ceases, and the watchdog physically disconnects the main relay after 120 seconds. This prevents the system from ever accidentally flattening the starter battery.

🗺️ Future Roadmap

  • Solar Integration: Adding an MPPT rail to allow the Nano to prioritize solar charging over the alternator when stationary.
  • Bluetooth Dashboard: Integrating an HC-05 module to stream live telemetry directly to a smartphone app, replacing the under-seat OLED display.
  • LiFePO4 Profiles: Expanding the software state-machine to include specific curves for Lithium Iron Phosphate batteries, which have much narrower voltage tolerances and require "cold-charge" protection.
